📈 Elevation profile

🛏️ Sleep strategy — sleep LOW, sleep SHELTERED. Even under the midnight sun the high passes sit near freezing at "night" with wind & snow patches — a sleeping-bag bivy up there is a real hypothermia risk. The route drops to warm valley bottoms at the right spots.

★ Primary: Øvre Årdal — km 740, 12 m (sea level). The best bivy on course: warmest air, sheltered fjord town, 24h services, and it sits right after Sognefjellet (hardest climb done) and before Valdresflye (fresh legs for it). At a fast pace you'd arrive ~18–22h in — ideal for a 2–4h sleep.
Sleeping ~2 h per 24 h? You'll need a chain of low, sheltered spots. Use these in order as your fatigue window lands — the pacing planner below auto-places a nap at the best one near each rest mark:
① Sunndalsøra (km 210, sea level) · ② Åndalsnes (km 380, sea level) · ③ Stranda (km 450, sea level — sheltered fjord town, 24h fuel & shops) · ④ Strynedalen (km 510, low valley — Stryn town is a 9 km W detour) · ⑤ Lom (km 640, 392 m — before Sognefjellet) · ★⑥ Øvre Årdal (km 740, 12 m — the prime spot) · ⑦ Vågåmo / Otta (km 930–940, ~370 m) · ⑧ Alvdal (km 1080, 596 m — last chance).
Golden rule still holds: always take your nap at the low valley town before a big cold pass, never after starting the climb.

Bivy micro-tips: sleep slightly up-slope (cold air & frost pool on the valley floor); use gapahuk lean-tos, rasteplass pull-offs, bus shelters or barn lees; get out of wind & under tree cover; reflective bivy bag over the sleeping bag. Right-to-roam (allemannsretten) applies on uncultivated land >150 m from houses.
⚠️ Cold high-pass danger zones — full kit, do NOT bivy: Sognefjellet (km 692, 1421 m · ~40 km no services) · Valdresflye (km 860, 1390 m) · Grotli/Strynefjellet (km 562, 1137 m) · Dovre/Grimsdalen (km 989, 1170 m, late & tired) · Tyin/Filefjell (km 764, 1076 m · a +1056 m climb straight off sea-level Øvre Årdal). 138 km of the route is above 1000 m. Carry food & warm/wind/rain layers into these — the biggest verified on-route gaps are Sunndalsøra→Vistdal (115 km, Aursjøvegen), Dovre→Alvdal (89 km, Dovrefjell), Bøverdalen→Årdal (83 km, Sognefjellet) and Hjelledalen→Bruvoll (72 km, Strynefjellet). 💧 Water & 🔧 repairs — quick reference

Water = basically a non-issue. Tap water is excellent & free everywhere; mountain streams above the farmland line are safe untreated and you'll cross hundreds. Refill at fuel stations, churches/cemeteries (outdoor taps), rasteplasser and DNT lodges. Carry ~1.5–2 L and top up often; a small filter is backup insurance for lowland/farm sections.

Repairs = be self-sufficient. Real bike shops only exist in the bigger towns (Tynset, Oppdal, Sunndalsøra, Åndalsnes, Stryn, Lom, Beitostølen, Vågå/Otta) and many keep short summer hours / shut at night. The hard middle third (Stryn→Lom→Årdal→Beitostølen) has the fewest options. Carry tubes/plugs, a spare derailleur hanger, quick-links, brake pads & sealant. (Exact shop names/hours not verified here — confirm the ones near your planned arrival times on Google Maps before the start.)

24h resupply: regular groceries run ~08:00–22:00 (shorter Sat) and are closed all day Sunday in Norway — which is your entire day 1. So between ~22:00–08:00, and the whole of Sunday, lean on 24h fuel (Circle K, Esso, YX, Uno-X), the race's unmanned supermarkets (scan bank card to enter Coop/Joker "døgnåpen" stores), and small Joker/Bunnpris <100 m² shops (allowed to open Sundays). Carry enough food from the start to cover day 1. Verified on-route 24h: Circle K E6 Otta (km 940, manned, hot food) & Circle K Alvdal (km 1066, 24/7); plus 24h shops Coop Prix Bøverdalen (km 658) & Bruvoll (km 609), both Mo–Sa (closed Sun). Most other shops shut 22:00–24:00 and all day Sunday — see the Resupply section below.
